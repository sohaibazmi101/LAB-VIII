## Real-World Datasets

A real-world dataset is a collection of data obtained from actual activities, systems, or observations. These datasets are widely used in data science for analysis, prediction, and decision-making.

Below are five examples of real-world datasets along with their descriptions:

---

### 1. Weather Dataset
A weather dataset contains information such as temperature, humidity, rainfall, wind speed, and atmospheric pressure collected over time from different locations.

**Usage:**
- Weather forecasting  
- Climate change analysis  
- Agriculture planning  

---

### 2. Healthcare Dataset
Healthcare datasets include patient details, medical history, diagnoses, treatments, and test results.

**Usage:**
- Disease prediction  
- Patient care improvement  
- Medical research and analysis  

---

### 3. E-commerce Sales Dataset
This dataset includes information about products, prices, customer purchases, transaction dates, and payment methods.

**Usage:**
- Sales analysis  
- Customer behavior analysis  
- Recommendation systems  

---

### 4. Social Media Dataset
Social media datasets contain data such as posts, likes, comments, shares, and user interactions from platforms like Twitter, Instagram, or Facebook.

**Usage:**
- Sentiment analysis  
- Trend detection  
- Social behavior studies  

---

### 5. Transportation Dataset
Transportation datasets include data related to traffic flow, vehicle counts, public transport schedules, and travel times.

**Usage:**
- Traffic management  
- Route optimization  
- Smart city planning  

---

## Conclusion
Real-world datasets help data scientists understand real-life problems and develop practical solutions by analyzing patterns and trends from actual data.
